Combat

In Brigandine, there is not merely one kind of combat, but four. They are as follows:

Skirmish
Used for quick resolutions, or for combat against weaker, unimportant foes.
Man-to-Man
Used to simulate in detail acts of swordplay and combat.
Jousting
A specialized system for choosing placement of strikes for single-combat duels
Fantasy Combat
For use against foes which can otherwise not be slain by mortal men

Any of these four may appear in the course of an adventure.

In general, all combat involves determining the attack and defensive strengths of each side (e.g. attk 2HF, def 1LC), determining the number of strikes each side may make, rolling the requisite dice (almost always ATK with melee and AIM with missile fire), then resolving successful strikes to determine victory.

Each system is explained below.

Arms and Armor

Arms

weapon class

the specific type of weapon: a longsword, a morning star, etc.

Skirmish Combat

This is the default combat system of the game, and serves as a baseline for all other combat.

Skirmish combat derives from wargame rules for units of several figures fighting. Units of troops are broken into either foot (infantry) or horse (cavalry), and further classified as light, medium, or heavy:

Foot Cavalry
Light LF infantry fighting in loose formation with basic weapons and limited gear. LC mounted soldiers mostly used for harrying opponents.
Medium MF infantry in closer formation with better weapons and moderate armor. MC mounted soldiers for direct assaults on enemy troops.
Heavy HF elite infantry units, able to withstand a cavalry charge. HC armored knights and shock troops to destroy enemy lines.

In Brigandine, a unit could be only a single soldier, but that single soldier may have the fighting strength of multiple men. The resolution is as follows:

  1. Determine the attacker's type and strength:
    • attk type --- whether LF,MF,HF,LC,MC,HC
    • #attk --- representing the effective number of men fighting
  2. Determine the defender's type:
    • def type --- whether LF,MF,HF,LC,MC,HC
    • #def --- representing the effective number of men defending
  3. Find cell in table corresponding to attacker/defender types. This cell lists the number of dice per man that the attacker will roll, followed by the necessary result for a success.
  4. The defender now finds his attk type/#
  5. Roll the total number of dice indicated, and count each success as a hit. Subtract these hits from the defender's hit count. The dice rolled are according to the ATK stat, unless otherwise specified.
