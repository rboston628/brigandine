Class Features

Blessings
A priest is able to say blessings. This is limited by his number of daily blessings. See below.
Exorcism
A priest may use his prayers and crucifix to attack certain creatues in Fantasy Combat as though armed with a magic sword:
  • lycanthropes (needs 9)
  • demons (needs 12)
  • wights (needs 6)
  • wraiths (needs 8)
Against non-FC undead monsters, the priest rolls 2d6 on the skill check table, using his level as the number of own figures, and can kill that many creatures. Certain holy relics may grant more own figures.
Set apart
A priest may not wield any weapon except the weapons of the spirit: a crucifix, his prayer book, and the sacraments. If he chooses to use a weapon in combat, he attacks as a peasant. He loses all daily blessings. Afterward he must spend an hour in repentance.
Oratio vulgaris
Priests can read and speak fluent Latin. This can facilitate easier communication with foreigners.

Starting Equipment

crucifix, robe, pyx box, prayer book.

Blessings

A priest is able to say any of the below blessings, subject to his daily limit.

Eucharist
The priest can consecrate holy eucharist. When administered to the soldiers, it removes any curse or negative spiritual effects. When touched to certain wicked abominations, it can attack FC-only monsters as though in Fantasy Combat (see Exorcism). This must be stored in a pyx box. If mishandled, the priest loses this abiity.
Unction
The priest may annoint another character before combat. During the next combat, this soldier will require simulataneous hits equal to his #def to take any damage.
Healing
The priest may touch one friendly character to restore on hit to that character's #def. This may be done to revive a character dead less than one exploration turn.
Visions
The priest will retreat into prayerful meditation to seek an answer to a question. For each hour spent in prayer, he rolls as that many number own figures on the skill check table. Depending on how high the result, he receives a spiritual answer.

Adapting to Other Deities

Because the setting is assumed to be Continental Europe in the Middle Ages, the priest is assumed to be Christian (either Roman or Byzantine, or by stretching the time period Celtic/Anglican or Lutheran). If it makes sense within the context of the adventure, the priest may alternately be an Islamic imam, a Jewish rabbi, or even a Zoroastrian magus, mutatis mutandis.

If the priest joins the party from a fantasy otherworld, this may be any other religion appropriate to that world.

This class is written with monotheism in mind. The priests of heathen gods are more warlike, but their deities also self-interested and amoral. The blessings will be only minor kick-backs in thanks for larger sacrifices. It is recommended heathen priests be handled more like elves.
